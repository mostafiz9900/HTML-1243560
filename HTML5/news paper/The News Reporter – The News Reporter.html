Event Details

A special service will be held at the Columbus County Sheriff ’s Office May 24 honoring local officers who died in the line of duty. As in past years, the sheriff ’s office is seeking relatives of all officers who paid the ultimate price. Family members will be escorted and honored during the event. A total of 11 local men have died in the line of duty since 1909. In that year, Constable Jesse Simon Long was shot and killed in Fair Bluff. Others include Bradd Cribb (1923); Hoke B. Smith (1930); Harry Long (1959) Milford Hardwick (1971); Lennue Hammond (1976); Troy Sigmon (1978); Robert “Bob” Hinson (1991); Andy Nobles (a local man on the Charlotte Police Department who died in 1993); Franklin Perritte (1995) and Timothy “Shane” Miller (2004). The 6 p.m. service will be held in front of the office, at the memorial near the flagpoles. Relatives of fallen Columbus County Law Enforcement Officers are asked to contact Susan Lennon at 910-642-6551 if they plan to attend.

Movies on Madison: Summer Film Series “Coco” Rescheduled for May 25

The North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences at Whiteville and the Columbus County Arts Council have partnered to bring you “Movies on Madison: Summer Film Series.” Kicking off the summer series is “Coco,” showing Friday, May 25 at dusk. From Walt Disney Pictures and Pixar Animation Studios, “Coco” takes viewers on an extraordinary journey with an aspiring young musician named Miguel to the magical land of his ancestors. A charming trickster named Hector becomes an unexpected friend who helps Miguel uncover the real story behind his family history. This Academy Award winner for best animated feature and best original song, “Coco” is a charming tale of family, fun and adventure that will warm your heart.

Dress in your favorite character’s costume. Bring a blanket or chair. Museum lawn opens at 6:00 p.m., movie starts at dusk. This free, 109-minute film is rated PG. This is a family-friendly event: no pets, coolers, alcohol or smoking permitted. Refreshments will be available for purchase. Visit our education table before the film.

This community event is made possible by the NC Museum of Natural Sciences at Whiteville and the Columbus County Arts Council. For more information, please call the museum at 910-914-4185 or email Whiteville@naturalsciences.org.

“Wings of Life” featured at Museum’s Science Cinema May 26

(WHITEVILLE, N.C.) — The North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences at Whiteville presents “Science Cinema,” Saturday, May 26, featuring Disneynature’s “Wings of Life.” This film takes a beautiful and unprecedented look at butterflies, hummingbirds, bees, bats and flowers and is a celebration of life, as a third of the world’s food supply depends on these amazing and increasingly threatened creatures. Incredible cinematography, high-speed, time lapse and microfilming making showcases in spectacular detail the unsung heroes of our planet.  

This 77-minute film will be shown at 10 a.m., noon and 2 p.m. The film is rated G. Admission is free. Science Cinema programming is funded by a grant from the International Paper Foundation.

To extend the learning experience of “Wings of Life” please visit our education table after the film. Science Cinema films are designed to educate and enlighten as much as entertain. A 26-page educator resource guide, designed for grades 2 through 4, extends the learning experience with lesson plans and activities tied to the film and can be downloaded for free at http://nature.disney.com/wings-of-life

For more information, please contact the museum at 910-914-4185 or Whiteville@naturalsciences.org.
